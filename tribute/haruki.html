Haruki Murakami

The Taciturn Writer



Here's some facts on this quiet writer's life:

  • 1949: Born in Kyoto, Japan
  • 1973: Graduted from Waseda University
  • 1974: Opened a jazz bar with his wife, Yoko
  • 1978: While watching a baseball game in Jingu Stadium, he became suddenly inspired to write a novel.
  • Autumn 1978: Finishes writing his first novel, "Hear the Wing Sing". Wins a new writer's contest.
  • 1980: Writes his second novel, "Pinball, 1973". The book was nominated for the Akutagawa Prize.
  • 1982: Writes his next book, "A Wild Sheep Chase". By this point, he sold his jazz bar and pursued writing full time.
  • 1985: Travels between Fujisawa and Sendagaya. Releases another book, "Hard Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World".
  • 1987: Writes "Norwegian Wood", which became extremely popular amongst young people, bringing him fame.
  • 1991: Moves to New Jersey and becomes an Associate Research at Princeton University
  • 1992: Promoted to Associate Professor at Princeton. "South of the Border, West of the Sun" was published this year.
  • 1993: Begins teaching at William Howard Taft University.
  • 1995: One his critically acclaimed novels, "The Wind Up Bird Chronicle", is published.
  • 1999: "Sputnik Sweetheart" is published.
  • 2002: Another critically acclaimed book, "Kafka on the Shore" is published.
  • 2005: The English translation of "Kafka on the Shore" is released. It becomes an instant hit.
  • 2009/2010: His magnum opus, "1Q84" is released in three volumes in Japan, which became a sensation upon release.
  • 2011: "1Q84" is translated into English.
  • 2013: "Colorless Tsukuru Tazaki and His Years of Pilgrimage" is released in Japan.
  • 2014: "Tsukuru Tazaki" is translated into English.

"There's no such thing as perfect writing, just like there's no such thing as perfect despair." - Haruki Murakami
